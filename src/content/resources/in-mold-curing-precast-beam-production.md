Curing is one of the most important process steps in precast beam production. It affects early strength development, production rhythm and the stability of later demolding and handling.

In a smart girder yard, curing should be considered as part of the production system, not as an isolated equipment item.

## What in-mold curing can support

In-mold curing can help the project team improve process consistency by keeping curing closer to the production line logic. This may reduce repeated handling and make production rhythm easier to manage.

The final configuration still depends on girder type, local climate, energy availability, project schedule and quality requirements.

## Energy options need comparison

Solar and air-source heat pump curing can be considered where the project values energy management and cleaner operation. Gas, biomass or other energy options may still be evaluated depending on local conditions.

Energy comparison should be presented with clear assumptions, including beam type, curing quantity, local energy price and operating schedule.
